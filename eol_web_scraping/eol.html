    The "poison oak," Toxicodendron diversilobum (Anacardiaceae) was seen in some of its many and varied forms: tree-enveloping vines, little baby seedlings, big woody deciduous shrubs (seen here, just leafing out), and big woody evergreen shrubs. Seen at Rockville Hills park, Solano Co., CA.

    No discussion of a hike in California would be complete without a caution about our Pacific poison-oak (Toxicodendron diversilobum). It is not a true oak, but a plant related to mangoes and pistachios. You know the drill: leaves of three, let them be.
